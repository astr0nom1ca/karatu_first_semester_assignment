Biography

My name is Oghenerukevwe Jeffrey Onemu and my AltSchool ID is: ALT/SOE/024/0167. I consider myself a very visual person. My early interests revolved around a fascination with the illustrations in cartoons and books , an eventual progression to recreating them and later on, making up my own illustrations. However, over time I lost interest in pursuing visual arts professionally and chose computer science as my course of study mostly because I felt that every other field was becoming a branch of computer science due to the far reaching applications of computer technology. My experience with programming has been rich and varied and it began in my second year of university when I had to write a C++ program to calculate Lagrange interpolation, a mathematical formula used to generate polynomials. I found the experience enlightening and highly fulfilling as prior to that assignment, I considered programming to be some unattainable skill and was frankly uninterested in it all together. However, being able to apply programming to solving a real world problem put it in a whole new light for me. This event would be what formally drew me into the world of programming and it’s many possibilities. From a surface level foray into online communities, I gleaned that Python was one of the less intimidating languages around which would lead me to various online tutorials and finally, a Sololearn course. The knowledge I acquired through independent learning came in handy during my third year when several of my courses required Python. It also put me far ahead of the required coursework and served as added motivation to pursue more knowledge. Since then, I have gained a strong understanding of HTML, responsive web design, PHP and Django through online courses. These courses made me much more aware of the front end engineering aspects of web development as well as the thought that goes into designs and the skill it takes to execute it. I now find myself silently rating websites by how easy they are to use, how well they display information, how attractive they are as a whole etc. This has increasingly led me to become re-interested in the visual arts. The only difference being that when I see a design I like now, I automatically wonder how something similar can be implemented on a web page.

Why I joined AltSchool Africa

I joined AltSchool Africa with the goal of acquiring comprehensive skills and in-depth knowledge in the field of web and application design. My primary focus is on creating responsive and mobile-friendly websites and applications that not only meet the current industry standards but also cater to the diverse needs of all potential users. I believe that in today’s digital landscape, it is essential to design interfaces that provide seamless user experiences across various devices. By participating in this program, I aim to learn best practices in user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design, ensuring that the products I create are accessible, intuitive, and visually appealing. Through AltSchool Africa, I hope to collaborate with like-minded individuals, gain insights from industry experts, and participate in hands-on projects that will solidify my skills and prepare me for a successful career in this ever-evolving field.

My goals for the FrontEnd Engineering Programme

My goals for the FrontEnd Engineering Program are multifaceted and deeply rooted in my passion for technology and design. First and foremost, I aspire to learn from the best that Africa has to offer, tapping into the wealth of knowledge and expertise available within this dynamic community of educators and industry leaders. I believe that learning from seasoned professionals and innovators will provide me with invaluable insights and skills that are crucial for my growth as a frontend engineer. Additionally, I am eager to meet new people and build meaningful connections with fellow participants who share my enthusiasm for frontend development. Furthermore, I am excited about the opportunity to design incredible projects that showcase my newfound skills and creativity. I aim to work on projects that not only demonstrate technical proficiency but also address real-world problems and enhance user experiences.
